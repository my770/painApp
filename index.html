Eyes

Unlike adults, most children requiring ophthalmic surgery will need a general anaesthetic. Operations involving the eye and orbit result in mild to moderate pain, which is well managed with topical anaesthetic agents and simple analgesics (paracetamol and NSAIDs)

Most children benefit from a level of sedation post operatively and strategies to reduce delirium. TIVA is ideal for ophthalmic surgery in paediatrics. However, for squint surgery you may experience more bradycardias as the resting heart rate is lower. If you decide to use cyclizine intraop the anticholinergic effect can be useful.

The surgery is often well tolerated with simple analgesics and fentanyl.

Avoid nitrous oxyide - They are associated with more severe postoperative pain and may require intraoperative morphine and post operative oromorph 0.1-0.2mg/kg 3 hourly.

Enucleations are often traumatic for the parents. A pre-med and gentle induction can help with these cases. Intra-op morphine is required (0.05mg/Kg), but the children have a high incidence of PONV. Consider intra-op clonidine 0.5-1mcg/kg.

Associated with a high incidence of postoperative nausea and vomiting. Double prophylactic antiemetic therapy intra-op is standard. Simple analgesia combined with clonidine and a reduced dose of morphine (for two or more muscle corrections) usually suffice.

The surgery requires a still eye. Muscular blockade, RAE tube and invasive ventilation provide ideal conditions. Care must be taken to reduce IOP at the end of surgery (aim to avoid crying and coughing). These patients benefit from clonidine and morphine.

This is a high turnover list. Paracetamol is sufficient for most procedures (Laser). If the surgeon uses cryo, then a small dose of fentanyl/morphine will be beneficial.

Neurosurgery

Most Tumours will require regular paracetamol, PRN oral morphine and local anaesthetic infiltration. Surgeons are happy with NSAIDs on day 1 postop. Regular ondansetron and PRN cyclizine. Clonidine can be given intra-op 1mcgr/Kg and continue PRN postop.

Post Fossa tumours will need NCA/PCA, Teenagers would benefit from Fentanyl or Fentanyl and Ketamine NCA/PCA

For sitting position, prolonged prone and positions where the head is turned to one side...children will benefit from diazepam 0.1mg/kg 8 hourly PRN for muscle spasms

Tunnelling can be painful, and dose of fentanyl 1mcgr/kg intra-operatively is usually enough. Regular paracetamol, ibuprofen and PRN oral-morphine 0.1mg/kg 3 hourly is usually enough in the postop period

Similar to VP shunts. There is no tunnelling involved, but a dose of fentanyl intra-op may be necessary. Regular paracetamol and ibuprofen prn postop if there are no contraindications, and very occasionally PO morphine 0.1mg/kg 6 hourly PRN

Usually neonates. May be a prolonged surgery requiring plastic surgery involvement Intraop can be managed with remifentanil or fentanyl boluses. Postop: if more than 38 weeks gestational age. Regular paracetamol 10mg/Kg tds IV (15mg/kg qds PO/PR) and morphine NCA (consider no background infusion).
